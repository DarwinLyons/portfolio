About

Hi! My name is Darwin Lyons. I’m an actor, director, creator, educator and producer in theatre and film. I make art in the hopes that audiences will be transported to a different world, and then be left with questions about what it means to be human. My favourite things are funny things, kind things, andinteresting things.

Creator

She Spreads (titled Dead Roads in the workshop phase) is a play created by Neema Bickersteth, Darwin Lyons, Clare Preuss, and Zoe Sweet. We won the Ontario Arts Council Phase 1 & 2 development grants and three creator’s reserve grants. A workshop of this show was part of the 2016 RISER Project and was then developed with The National Arts Center’s October Oasis and Factory Theatre’s Residency Program. Educator

Shakespeare In The Ruff, Younth Coordinator

I am currently the Youth Development & Outreach Coordinator for the Young Ruffian Apprenticeship Program, a 5-week free of charge summer theatre training program for youth ages 13-19. Click here to find out more.

The Banff Citadel Training Program

I worked with Ravi Jain as the Associate Director of the Banff Citadel Training program. Ravi and his company, Why Not Theatre, curated the 2018/19 program. The Program participants stared in a reimagining of Shakespeare's The Tempest, directed by Josette Bushell-Mingo, former Artistic Director of Tyst Teater, Sweden’s National Deaf Theatre. The Program offers an advanced creative development program to enrich the performance skills of established theatre professionals. The 2019 program featured Deaf and Hearing actors.

RAE, Young People's Theatre

I was a Resident Artist Educator for Young People's Theatre for the 2017/18 and 2018/19 seasons. The Resident Artist Educator program at YPT furthers the theatre’s leadership in the field of arts education. As both artists and educators, each participant creates, teaches and learns during their residency thereby embodying the company’s dual identity as a professional theatre and centre for arts education.

Producer

The Paprika Festival, Artistic Producer

From 2015-2017 I worked for the Paprika Festival as the Artistic Producer. Paprika is a year-round theatre training program and festival for youth. While there I was part of the creation of the first-ever strategic plan. I created a new program, The Director’s Lab. While there Paprika won the NOW Magazine Reader’s Choice award for best youth organization.
